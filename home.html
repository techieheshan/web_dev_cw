Our Mission

Why we're doing this

Same Uniform, Different Chances

Two students can sit in the same classroom, wear the same uniform, and still end up with wildly different opportunities — because one of them has books, wifi, and someone to ask for help at home, and the other doesn't. That gap is what we're actually trying to close.

  • Share study resources with peers who lack them
  • Volunteer as a peer tutor
  • Support digital-access initiatives on campus

You Don't Need to Wait Until Graduation

Honestly, none of us thought we could do much about this until we actually tried. Turns out lending your old notes to a first-year or spending an hour a week tutoring adds up faster than you'd expect — you don't need a degree or a budget to start.

  • Mentor a first-year student
  • Donate unused textbooks and supplies
  • Join or start a study support group

This Is What We're Actually Working Toward

This is the moment all of this is for. Not a statistic, an actual person crossing the stage who almost didn't get the chance to. If a handful of us at Westminster keep doing the small things, more of these moments happen for people who deserve them.

  • Higher retention among supported students
  • Stronger peer networks
  • A culture where knowledge-sharing is normal, not exceptional
